# We Tested Four Newsletter Platforms So You Don't Have To

Six months ago our team did something mildly unhinged: we ran our actual company newsletter on four different platforms simultaneously, splitting our list four ways, just to see which tool deserved to keep the job. Here's the honest report.

The contenders were Beehiiv, ConvertKit, Mailerlite, and Buttondown — chosen because they're the four we get asked about most. Our list at the start of the test sat at 24,000 subscribers, split evenly, and we published twice weekly for the full six months.

Let's talk money first, because that's where the differences bite. For a list of 6,000 (each platform's share), Beehiiv came out cheapest at $49 per month on its Scale plan, with Mailerlite close behind at $54. ConvertKit — now rebranded as Kit — landed at $79, and Buttondown, at $90 for that volume, was the priciest of the four, though it's worth noting its pricing favors smaller lists dramatically.

Deliverability told a different story. Buttondown quietly posted the best inbox placement of the group: 97% of test sends landed in the primary inbox across our seed accounts, with Kit at 95%, Beehiiv at 93%, and Mailerlite trailing at 89% — enough of a gap that we noticed it in open rates before we ever ran the formal test.

On the editor experience, Beehiiv won our writers' hearts with its speed, though two of them noted its analytics felt "designed to make numbers look exciting rather than useful." Kit's automation builder remains the most powerful of the four — if your newsletter is really a funnel in disguise, that's your tool. Mailerlite surprised us with the best multilingual support, which matters for our Spanish-language edition.

The verdict? We consolidated onto Beehiiv for the main newsletter, kept Buttondown for our small premium digest where deliverability is everything, and said grateful goodbyes to the other two. Total migration time was about two weekends. Would we run this experiment again? Ask us after the therapy.
