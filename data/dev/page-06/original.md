# How Marisol Home Turned Abandoned Carts into Their Best Sales Channel

When Valentina Reyes took over ecommerce at Marisol Home, a mid-sized furniture retailer with stores across three Mexican cities and a growing online shop, she inherited a problem hiding in plain sight: beautiful traffic numbers, painful conversion numbers.

"We were spending real money getting people to the site," Valentina told us over coffee, "and then watching seventy percent of the carts just... evaporate."

## Finding the leak

The team's first instinct — cut prices, add popups — felt wrong to Valentina. Instead, she spent six weeks just measuring. The diagnosis: cart abandonment at Marisol was running at 74%, notably above the furniture-industry benchmark her team pegged at 68%. Worse, the store had no recovery mechanism at all. An abandoned cart was simply a goodbye.

## The unglamorous fix

What followed wasn't a redesign or a rebrand. It was a three-email recovery sequence, built over two sprints with their agency. The first email goes out one hour after abandonment — a simple "still thinking it over?" with a photo of the exact items left behind. The second, at 24 hours, adds answers to the four questions their support team hears most (delivery times, assembly, returns, and financing). Only the third email, at 72 hours, includes any incentive: free white-glove delivery rather than a discount, a deliberate choice to protect margins.

## What happened

Within the first full quarter, the sequence was recovering 11% of abandoned carts — money that previously vanished. Because the average order value at Marisol sits around $340, that translated into roughly $58,000 in recovered revenue per month. The white-glove offer, it turns out, converts nearly twice as well as the 10% discount they tested against it, while costing the business less.

"The lesson," Valentina says, "is that people weren't leaving because of price. They were leaving because of doubt. Answer the doubt and they come back."

Marisol Home now sends every new hire in marketing this case study on their first day.
